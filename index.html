Common Diseases in Nigeria

Malaria

What is it? A life-threatening disease caused by parasites transmitted through mosquito bites.

Symptoms:

  • High fever and chills
  • Severe headache
  • Body aches and fatigue
  • Nausea and vomiting

Prevention:

  • Sleep under insecticide-treated nets
  • Use mosquito repellents
  • Keep environment clean and drain stagnant water
  • Take antimalarial drugs as prescribed

Typhoid Fever

What is it? A bacterial infection spread through contaminated food and water.

Symptoms:

  • Prolonged high fever
  • Weakness and stomach pain
  • Headache and loss of appetite
  • Constipation or diarrhea

Prevention:

  • Drink only clean, treated water
  • Wash hands regularly with soap
  • Eat properly cooked food
  • Get typhoid vaccination

Hypertension (High Blood Pressure)

What is it? A condition where blood pressure is consistently too high.

Risk Factors:

  • Excessive salt intake
  • Obesity and lack of exercise
  • Stress and family history
  • Smoking and excessive alcohol

Management:

  • Regular blood pressure monitoring
  • Reduce salt intake
  • Exercise regularly
  • Take prescribed medications
  • Maintain healthy weight

Maternal & Child Health

Antenatal Care

Pregnant women should attend at least 8 antenatal visits:

  • First visit: Before 12 weeks of pregnancy
  • Regular check-ups throughout pregnancy
  • Blood tests and ultrasound scans
  • Nutrition counseling and supplements
  • Tetanus vaccination

Breastfeeding

Benefits:

  • Provides complete nutrition for baby
  • Protects against infections
  • Promotes bonding between mother and child
  • Reduces risk of childhood diseases

Recommendations:

  • Exclusive breastfeeding for first 6 months
  • Continue breastfeeding up to 2 years and beyond
  • Introduce complementary foods after 6 months

Child Development Milestones

0-6 months: Smiles, rolls over, reaches for objects

6-12 months: Sits without support, crawls, says first words

1-2 years: Walks independently, speaks short sentences

2-3 years: Runs, climbs, speaks in sentences

Note: If your child is not meeting milestones, consult a healthcare provider.

Nutrition & Wellness

Balanced Diet

A healthy diet should include:

  • Carbohydrates: Rice, yam, cassava, bread, plantain
  • Proteins: Beans, fish, meat, eggs, milk
  • Vitamins & Minerals: Fruits and vegetables (especially leafy greens)
  • Water: At least 8 glasses daily

Foods to Limit

  • Excessive salt and sugary drinks
  • Fried and fatty foods
  • Processed foods with preservatives
  • Excessive alcohol

Healthy Living Tips

  • Exercise at least 30 minutes daily
  • Get 7-8 hours of sleep
  • Manage stress through relaxation
  • Regular medical check-ups
  • Avoid tobacco and limit alcohol

Vaccination Schedule

Childhood Immunization

At Birth:

  • BCG (Tuberculosis)
  • Hepatitis B (1st dose)
  • Oral Polio Vaccine

6 Weeks:

  • Pentavalent (DPT, Hepatitis B, Hib) - 1st dose
  • Oral Polio Vaccine - 1st dose
  • Pneumococcal vaccine - 1st dose

10 Weeks:

  • Pentavalent - 2nd dose
  • Oral Polio Vaccine - 2nd dose
  • Pneumococcal vaccine - 2nd dose

14 Weeks:

  • Pentavalent - 3rd dose
  • Oral Polio Vaccine - 3rd dose
  • Pneumococcal vaccine - 3rd dose

9 Months:

  • Measles vaccine
  • Yellow Fever vaccine

Adult Vaccinations

  • Tetanus booster every 10 years
  • Hepatitis B (if not vaccinated)
  • Influenza (annual for elderly)
  • COVID-19 vaccination

Mental Health

Understanding Mental Health

Mental health is just as important as physical health. It affects how we think, feel, and act.

Common Mental Health Conditions

Depression:

  • Persistent sadness and loss of interest
  • Changes in sleep and appetite
  • Difficulty concentrating
  • Feelings of hopelessness

Anxiety:

  • Excessive worry and fear
  • Restlessness and tension
  • Rapid heartbeat
  • Difficulty sleeping

Maintaining Good Mental Health

  • Talk to friends and family
  • Exercise regularly
  • Get enough sleep
  • Eat healthy meals
  • Practice relaxation techniques
  • Seek professional help when needed

When to Seek Help

Seek professional help if you experience:

  • Prolonged sadness or anxiety
  • Difficulty functioning daily
  • Substance abuse problems
  • Thoughts of self-harm

First Aid

Basic First Aid Kit

Every home should have:

  • Sterile bandages and gauze
  • Adhesive tape and plasters
  • Antiseptic solution
  • Pain relievers (paracetamol)
  • Thermometer
  • Scissors and tweezers
  • Disposable gloves

Common Emergencies

Cuts and Wounds:

  • Wash hands before treating
  • Clean wound with clean water
  • Apply pressure to stop bleeding
  • Cover with sterile bandage
  • Seek medical help for deep cuts

Burns:

  • Cool the burn with running water (10-20 minutes)
  • Do not apply ice directly
  • Cover with clean cloth
  • Do not burst blisters
  • Seek medical help for serious burns

Choking:

  • Encourage coughing if person can breathe
  • Perform back blows (5 times)
  • Perform abdominal thrusts if needed
  • Call for emergency help

Fainting:

  • Lay person flat on back
  • Elevate legs above heart level
  • Loosen tight clothing
  • Check breathing and pulse
  • Seek medical help if not recovering
